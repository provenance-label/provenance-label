What is this?

The Provenance Label is an open standard for disclosing AI collaboration in creative work.

Just like nutrition labels tell you what's in your food without judging your choices, Provenance Labels show what's in your content without claiming it's good or bad.

It's not anti-AI. It's pro-honesty.

Why it exists

A reader recently told me my AI-assisted writing felt "bloated and self-righteous." They were right to spot it. But they deserved to know upfront.

We made this label because:

  • AI collaboration is here to stay
  • Transparency builds trust
  • Readers/users deserve to know
  • Creators deserve a simple, non-defensive way to disclose

Like B-Corp certification but actually simple. Like a nutrition label but for content.

How to use it

1. Start with intent

At the beginning of your AI session, say:

"Let's use Provenance Label standards. Track our collaboration and generate the label when we're done."

The AI will track contribution mix, decisions, and edits throughout your work.

2. Generate the label

When finished, ask:

"Generate the Provenance Label"

The AI calculates percentages based on your session. You review and approve or adjust.

3. Add it to your work

Bottom of blog posts, README files, artist statements, wherever makes sense.

4. That's it

No approval needed. No fees. It's an open standard.

The Standard (v1.0)

PROVENANCE LABEL v1.0 Created: YYYY-MM-DD HH:MM UTC Updated: YYYY-MM-DD HH:MM UTC (optional) Edited: Yes/No Human contribution: [0-100%] AI contribution: [0-100%] AI models used: [model names] Process notes: [1-2 sentence description] Adjustments: [if Edited=Yes, explain changes]

How it works:

1. AI tracks collaboration in real-time
2. AI calculates contribution percentages
3. Human reviews and approves (or adjusts)
4. Label gets added to final work

Rules:

  • AI-calculated, human-approved
  • Percentages should add to 100%
  • Process notes in plain language
  • Free to use, modify, distribute

Generate Your Label

Copy this prompt into your AI at the end of your collaboration:

Create a Provenance Label v1.0 for the content we produced in this conversation.

        Format:
        **Provenance Label v1.0**
        - Human Contribution: [X]%
        - AI Contribution: [Y]%
        - Collaboration Method: [description]
        - AI Tool(s): [your name and version]
        - Human Roles: [specific actions taken]
        - AI Roles: [specific actions taken]

        Requirements:
        - Calculate percentages based on actual contribution (ideation, drafting, editing, decision-making)
        - Be specific about methods (prompting, iterative editing, research, code generation)
        - List concrete roles, not abstractions
        - Output only the label, ready to copy-paste

FAQ

Is this anti-AI?

No. It assumes AI collaboration can be valuable when disclosed honestly.

How does the AI calculate percentages?

The AI tracks who initiated content, who made edits, who drove key decisions, and what percentage of final output came from each contributor. It's algorithmic but not perfect—human approval is the final step.

What if I'm working with an AI that doesn't track this automatically?

Ask it to track manually: "Let's use Provenance Label standards." Most modern AIs can track collaboration if prompted. For existing work, you can estimate and use this generator.

Do the percentages have to be exact?

No. The AI estimates based on session data. You adjust if needed. Aim for honest, not perfect.

Can I modify the label format?

Yes. It's open. But if you change the format significantly, version it (v1.1, v2.0) or call it something else.

Does this protect against plagiarism?

No. Provenance Labels disclose the collaboration process between human and AI, but don't verify that contributions are original. Think of it like a nutrition label—it tells you what's in the product, not whether the ingredients are ethically sourced. Use plagiarism detection tools separately if content verification is needed.

Can Provenance Labels be faked?

Yes. Like any self-disclosure system, it relies on creator integrity. Your reputation is on the line.

What if the AI and I disagree on the percentages?

You have final say. Adjust the numbers, mark as "Edited: Yes" and explain in Adjustments field.
